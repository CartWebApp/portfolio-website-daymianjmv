Gaming

Some of my favorite games are mix of casual, yet, competive creativity-fueled adventures in Minecraft, Roblox, and first person shooter (FPS) game of Kirka.io. Each of these games feeds a different part of my brain—Minecraft scratches the builder/designer itch, Roblox fuels endless experimentation, and Kirka is where the competitive spirit really kicks in.


These games aren’t just hobbies—they’ve shaped how I think about design, interactivity, and even teamwork. Whether I’m strategizing in a fast-paced FPS match, building a redstone contraption that definitely won’t explode (probably), or exploring the endless creativity of player-made worlds, I’m always learning, iterating, and having a little too much fun while I’m at it.

Coding

I like to code websites for fun. Yes, for fun. While some people unwind with a good book or a Netflix binge, I relax by wrangling CSS and chasing down the one div that refuses to behave. I’ve built websites just for the joy of it, whether it’s for friends, community projects, or the occasional “hey, wouldn’t it be cool if…” idea that spirals into a full-blown site at 2 a.m.


From clean landing pages to quirky community hubs, I’ve put together a variety of sites—usually for free, because sometimes the best payment is the reaction of someone seeing their idea live on the web (and maybe a few memes shared in the process).
